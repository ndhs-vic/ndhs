NEWSLETTER FEBRUARY 2008

 

MARKET

Yes, it’s that time of year again! Our March market is on Saturday 15th March 8.30 – noon at Rotunda Park Newstead. This time we will be without Giselle and John Neall so we will all have to shuffle along to fill the very big space that these volunteers leave. (All our good wishes for the birth of the new grandchild in WA, Giselle and John). So any help from our members will be especially appreciated. Our raffle is for a homemade fruitcake decorated for Easter. We don’t send out raffle tickets for our March Market as we feel once a year is enough to approach our members in this regard but if anyone feels that they could sell some tickets please contact me (Dawn 54762006).

 

BROCHURES

The five brochures that Liz Coady produced in 2000 are in need of updating. Work has already gone into changing the text, printing out copies and proofreading. We hope to soon be in position to have them printed and available to be distributed to the various outlets in the district.

 

BOOKCASE

You will no doubt remember our book auction last year of the wonderful collection of books left to us by Miss Adelaide Mackie. The society retained a small library of books that we considered would be of use to us and also some that reflects the interests of our benefactor. A decision was made to get a glass-fronted bookcase in which to house the collection. This has been made to our specifications by John Earl of Maryborough and the result is most pleasing. There is now the need to get a small plaque made to attest to the source of both the books and the bookcase – Miss Mackie.

 

ANNUAL MEETING

This takes place at our April meeting – at 8 pm on Monday night April 20th. As has been our tradition, we will have a guest speaker. This year we are delighted that Mellissa Conn will talk about their home in Guildford – the building known in former times as the goldfields Gallery, London House or Sinclair’s. Mellissa and Jason have found out a lot about its history and also mush evidence from previous owners of the building as they restore the building and dig in the garden. They have arranged the collection in the front window of the shopfront. Of course this will also reflect the life and times of Guildford itself. I should be a most interesting evening. It will also be the night when we present out annual reports and elect office bearers for 2008 – 2009.

 

 

On a recent Newstead walk there was interest from some visitors at ‘Newstead Live’ in the old mill. With this in mind, included in this newsletter are some information and old images.

 

From “The Flour Mills of Victoria 1840 - 1990” by Lewis and Peggy Jones

 

Newstead, Sheehans’ Flour Mill 1869

J C Sheehan and family erected a new flour mill in Layard Street, Newstead in 1869. The tree-storied building was of bluestone, with bluestone stables at the rear. The building operations were in the hands of Thomas Trewick and it was reported on April 27, 1869, that the mill was expected to open in a few weeks, which it apparently did. It was called the “Victoria Flour Mill”. The mill processed all types of grain and made several kinds of meal. Repairs or improvements were done in 1883/85, according to Boddington’s Journal.

In 1914 the mill was forced to close. It could not be sold as a whole, so the roofing was sold and the interior fittings removed. Metal from old flour mills was commonly sold for wartime scrap during World War I; undoubtable some of the “Victoria’s” equipment contributed to the war effort also.

The building fell into a bad state of disrepair during the years that followed. Members of the family continued to live in the nearby brick residence until 1970. The mill was taken over and converted into a restaurant during the 1970s; it was called the “Lion and Piper Inn”; with “a peaceful Scottish flavour…the peaceful Loddon flows past giving the establishment about 182 metres of river frontage”. Repairs and rebuilding had been somewhat difficult due to a large peppercorn tree which had grown inside the shell of the mill.
