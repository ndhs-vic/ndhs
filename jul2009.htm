                   JULY 2009 NEWSLETTER

 

EXCURSION

It has been some considerable time since a visit has been made to our neighbours at Carisbrook to see their collection and archives. In that time they have achieved a great deal and at our last meeting it was thought that it would be good if another visit could be arranged. As it happened, in the next few days, four of our members attended their annual dinner and Show and Tell night at Caroline’s Restaurant, Carisbrook (always a most popular event), and Alex Stoneman was able to confirm that the date of August 10th would be suitable. The arrangements are to car pool from our courthouse at 1.30 pm and to help Carisbrook with their arrangements, could you contact me – Dawn Angliss 54762006.

 

PARTY

Winter is here and that is a reminder to organise our annual get-together. On Mondays, we assist visitors with their enquiries and generally get on with our various individual projects. A few years ago a decision was made to use our wonderful venue for a social event to indulge ourselves. Those who attend bring some food and drink to share and in the warm, welcoming atmosphere of the courthouse, we congratulate ourselves on another year’s effort. An invitation is extended to all our members, both local and distant, to join us on the night of Saturday 29th August at 6.30 pm.

 

A MARKS CONNECTION

In an article in a recent newsletter, attention was drawn to Richard and Phillipa Marks who were Newstead residents in the years prior to 1900. At our last meeting, Alan Burgess, long-time Newstead resident and our previous president, explained that he was a distant relative and his father was named Richard after Richard Marks – Richard Burgess being born in 1898 the year that that worthy citizen, Richard Marks, died.

VALE JOAN CULVENOR

Our society has been saddened by the death of one of our life members – Joan Culvenor. Joan was a former secretary of the Historical Society and her professionally typed copies of letters in our files are testament to how well she carried out her duties.

Joan grew up in Sandon at her parent’s farm, ‘Merlyn’ and went to school in Newstead. By then the Sandon school opened and she did another two years to get her Merit certificate and continued on doing her secondary schooling by correspondence until year 10 when she travelled to Castlemaine High to complete her schooling. This involved riding a bicycle into Newstead to catch the quarter to seven train. After school the train left Castlemaine at 5 pm and then Joan had to ride the three miles home – leaving and returning in the dark in winter. After her marriage to Alex Culvenor their life included spells of working in Melbourne as well as overseas stints in England and Washington DC. In retirement it was to return to Sandon again, this time to ‘Osnaburg’, the farm that both Joan and Alex’s families had been connected with.

Her knowledge of the district was formidable and she willingly shared it with newcomers. In recent years Joan participated in an oral history project and it is comforting to know that many of her memories of life in Sandon and Newstead have been preserved.

 

Joan Culvenor, Secretary, Ross Suter, President.
