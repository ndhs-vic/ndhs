NEWSLETTER APRIL 2008

 

ANNUAL MEETING

After the election of office bearers, Mellissa Conn spoke enthusiastically about the history of the former Goldfields Gallery Guildford. Mellissa and her husband Jason bought the property two years ago and have been doing research into the story of the buildings and their former owners as well as collecting various interesting items that have surfaced during their restorations or garden excavations.

The first owner was William Spinks; partner with Israel Kirkpatrick who had a soap factory in Short Street. When Spinks decided to retire to England, he sold the property to a John Sinclair. Photos show the store being called London House, a name that was replicated in many towns at that time. Sinclair married Elizabeth Church sister of Giles Church who built the music hall next door. At this time it became a telegraph office and Sinclair was made a Justice of the Peace. At this time too there was trouble with the run-off of water into the cellar from the road that was higher than the store. However efforts to get compensation went as far as Redmond Barry in the Victorian Supreme Court but to no avail. Sinclair died in 1885 and Elizabeth rented the store to James and Louisa Ellis in the 1890s before they relocated to the post office around the corner.

Elizabeth went on to marry firstly, John Smith and then John Myring.

In 1907, David Galbraith McIlenney became a butcher and moved into London House. and bought the property from a J Bell.

In 1922 the Passalaqua family continued the butchering business from there and also lived on site. Another owner since then has been David Syme in the 1960s and in the 1970s it became Goldfields Gallery.

Mellissa had photos on her laptop to add to the interesting story and at the end gave thanks to the sources of her information – BDM’s, newspaper articles, and Ray Bradfield’s book, Frank Passalaqua, Ray Pattle and the Newstead & District Historical Society for their database.

Janet Trudgeon thanked Mellissa for her talk and the attendees enjoyed supper while they looked at the tray of items found on site that Mellissa had brought along.

 

HERITAGE FESTIVAL

Brochures are available from Mount Alexander Shire outlining the activities that are happening in May under the heading of Law and Order. In Newstead the courthouse will be open on Sunday 18th May from 2 – 4 pm. Other activities are many and varied and include walks, exhibitions and addresses in the different towns.

 

EXCURSION

Now that the hot weather has gone, it seems time to have another excursion. Eddy Ford will take us along the Muckleford Gorge on Monday 12th May leaving from the courthouse at 1.30pm. This will entail a walk of about 3 kilometres but those who have been privileged to have been there before can really recommend this. As it is on private land, it is only on occasions such as this that there is a chance eo walk along a wonderful stretch of country bounded on one side by the Guildford plateau with its adits into the hillside and the stretches of creek with waterholes and steep rocky edges.

 

CALENDAR

Monday 12th May excursion to Muckleford Gorge 1.30 pm from the courthouse

Sunday 18th May display “Law & Order” at courthouse 2 – 4 pm

Monday 19th  May meeting at courthouse 1.30 pm
