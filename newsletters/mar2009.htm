MARCH 2009 NEWSLETTER

 

MARKET

The newsletter has been delayed a week to be able to give a report on the March Market which was held last Saturday – 21st March. We are happy to report that the weather was kind and the treasurer, Janet Trudgeon, banked about $900 out of which accounts for the advertising still are outstanding. This was our best March market result and buoys us up continue for some time to come. One of the stall holders asked how long the markets had been going and research by our treasurer found evidence that the first one was on 5th October 1989 which means that they have been going for 20 years. Susanne Redlande won the raffle for the Easter fruitcake and the lucky stallholder was Ron from Werribee. This year a couple of local boys were busking with their music and we are grateful to them for adding to the atmosphere on the day. The PA system set up in the Rotunda itself continues thanks to Karen Pierce who is carrying on the work that her father Dave Clark did for so many years. The “team Market” by now have the jobs allotted and it all seems to come together on the day – from the guys who put up the awning and clean the BBQ on Friday, the cooks, those who bake the goodies to sell, the guy who brings and returns all the white elephant boxes from the shed at the courthouse, the person who puts up and takes down the sign at the town’s entrance, those who arrange to get the other sign to our sign writer, the traditional tea and coffee maker who brings all the chairs, tables and washing up equipment, the person who goes around the stallholders to collect the $5s and issue flyers for next market, the work of advertising the market ably done in recent times by Cherie Lawton, Jim Trudgeon who arranges for the trestles to be borrowed from the Uniting Church and transports them, Janet Trudgeon who gets the ‘float’ and banks the money and not forgetting Giselle Neall’s efforts to organise all the food part of the operation.

Giselle’s efforts include ordering the meat and picking it up, buying bread, cutting up onions, making coleslaw, buying cans of drinks at bargain prices during the year, chilling and transporting them and all the other eskies, nailing up notices of prices, baking the cakes for the raffle and stallholders and presenting them most attractively, keeping records of how each section performs every market, checking takings and overseeing the important food handling guide lines.

Thank you to all.

EXCURSION

The Labour Day Monday was a busy one for the society. In the morning a working bee made sure that grass was cut and the inside of the courthouse was swept and dusted and silverware cleaned. Since then the accumulation of excess items on the main table has also been cleared, mulch obtained “gratis” and spread around our plantings and the result is that the place is looking at its best.

In the afternoon several cars met with our leader Derek Reid at the junction of the Sandy Creek Road and the Maldon – Castlemaine Road which was where gold was discovered in Sandy Creek in1855. From then the search for gold followed the creek south and so did we. The next stop was the Prince Llewellyn mine site where some of the original bob-pit stonework was still visible as well as a quite substantial tailings dump. Further on the remains of Druer’s blacksmith establishment could be seen  - an outbuilding, the old mud-built house and some garden plants. The site of Edison’s store had been rebuilt on. The site of the Shamrock Hotel was on the corner of Sell’s Road and the Newstead – Maldon Road. Nearby the Sandy Creek school had closed being replaced by a school in nearby Welshmans Reef, to where most of the population had moved. Eventually even that closed and it can now be seen in its new role as the guide hall near the Castlemaine railway station. The Wesleyan Church in Welshmans Reef also closed and moved. It is the wooden building behind the brick former church in Winters Flat.

 

ANNUAL MEETING

Our annual meeting will be held on Monday 20th April at the courthouse at 7.30 pm. The speaker for the night will be Alex Culvenor, who grew up in the Sandon area and will be able to tell us his story as it relates to our area and also that of the Culvenor family who were some of the very early settlers to the district. We are looking forward very much to this occasion and invite our members and others to join us. The short meeting to elect office bearers will be held first and the talk will be followed by supper.
