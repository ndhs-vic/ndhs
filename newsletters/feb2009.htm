FEBRUARY 2009 NEWSLETTER

 

 EXPLANATION

Last month’s newsletter had a change of format. After attending a seminar run by RHSV and in an attempt to make the appearance of the letter a little more professional, information such as committee members and details of our fee structure was included. Some of our members thought that fees were due and sent their monies. We have credited you with membership for 2009 – 2010 and apologise for any inconvenience. With a bit of help, work on a different format continues!

 

EXCURSION

Hopefully the extremes of weather will have gone as we propose to start our  excursions next month. On Monday 9th March it is proposed that we visit the Welshmans Reef area and our leader will be our president and resident of Welshmans Reef, Derek Reid. The arrangements will be as usual  - carpooling from the courthouse at 1.30 pm. We always include an afternoon tea and so we ask those who attend to bring a cup and a plate to share.

 

WORKING BEE

On Monday morning March 9th we intend to have a working be. The main job will be to spread mulch around the planting of trees and shrubs surrounding the new toilet and shed. We have been amazed at the growth the plants have shown since their planting last winter and the mulch will help the plants and improve the look of this part of our property. The surrounds of the courthouse have been kept in good order mainly thanks to John Neall’s work on our mower. The shed and toilet are proving a wonderful asset.

 

READER PRINTER

There has been a deal of excitement to have our new Scan Pro 1,000 installed at last. It has proved easy to use and there is quite a competition to be able get to use it on a Monday morning. It is in use for members who are reading and indexing the local newspapers, by Janet Trudgeon who submits the very popular items in the monthly ECHO community paper with news of a hundred years ago and it will come into great use by the various visitors who come to research their family history.

 

THOMAS MARTIN LETTER

Most of the local members are familiar with the Thomas Martin letter but it may need an explanation for others. There has been in circulation in Newstead, a 19 page letter written by a Thomas Martin which begins by saying “ As I promised to give you a few details of Newstead in the early days ….” He then proceeds to give a wonderful glimpse of life in Newstead and Strangways from when his family settled in 1858. Our society previously photocopied the letter in a fairly amateurish way and sold it. However one of our members Liz Coady has worked to provide a much improved version with Thomas’s story and research into many of the things that are mentioned in his letter. The society is in the process of having this published and look forward to announcing details of the launch later this year.

The Letter has been dramatised and has been presented in Newstead on a fairly regular basis. The next dramatisation will be at Dig Café Newstead in April. One of our members has been involved in a display of photos to accompany this performance. Gail Leech has done a wonderful job of putting together enhanced copies of old photographs from our collection and from local families and will display these at Dig along side photos of the sites today. Copies of the photos will be for sale. The opening of Gail’s exhibition will be at Dig café at 4 pm on Saturday 28th March at 4 pm. and will be launched by the “mayor of Newstead” (aka Steve Walter). Everyone is welcome.

 

MARKET

It is amazing how quickly the time goes between November and March. It seems we have only cleared away the things from the November Market and we are getting ready for our March one. Saturday March 21st is the date and Rotunda Park is the venue. If anyone can help in person on the day, or by dropping in cakes, biscuits or produce to sell, we would be most grateful.
