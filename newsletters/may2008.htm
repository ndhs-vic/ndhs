NEWSLETTER MAY 2008

 

SUBSCRIPTIONS

Our treasurer, Janet Trudgeon, is most impressed with the payment of subscriptions by members and passes on her thanks for your prompt payment.

 

EXCURSION

Unfortunately our leader was unable to take us to the eagerly awaited walk to Muckleford Gorge. Perhaps it will be possible to do it in spring. However we did manage to put together an outing at the last minute. By carpooling we were able to walk and drive along the route outlined in the brochure ‘Sacred to Worship and Memory’. We started by driving to the Avenue of Honour, established in 1919 to recognise the 80 plus men from this area who enlisted for World War 1. According to the brochure plane trees were planted but as most of the trees seem to be elms, it was presumed that a lot have had to be replaced over the years. Unfortunately details of which tree was allotted to which soldier is not known at this stage. The next stop was beside the wonderful old Red gum in Tivey Street where a plaque has been erected to record this as the site of the first church service. However the date of 1837 was thought not to be correct. The War Memorial in the park opposite the Railway Hotel was the next stop. Here it was noted that more than 40 names were listed as giving their lives n the Great War – about half of those who went. A walk then took us to both St Ann’s Catholic Church and the former St Andrew’s Presbyterian Church, now privately owned. After returning to the courthouse walks were taken to see the Anglican and Uniting Churches. Thanks to those who had keys and allowed us see inside our 3 churches. At our afternoon tea at the courthouse it was suggested that a similar excursion might be to visit the sites of the former hotels around Newstead.

DISPLAY

As already publicised, a display has been put together at the courthouse with the theme of Law and Order. This shows how a courthouse and police presence came to be established in Newstead. Several interesting newspaper articles are on display as well as a photo and documentation of the career of Mounted Constable Stanley Ellis who was stationed at Newstead about the time of the First World War. Also shown is a photo of Chief Commissioner Sainsbury who Newstead was proud to claim as having grown up here. There is the story of an early gruesome murder as well as other lesser misdemeanours reported in the local papers. The display will continue to be on view for some time.

 

PRYOR BLACKSMITH SITE

An article in last month’s Echo produced a lot of response. We were able to confirm the site as being on the northeast corner of Simpson Street and the Ballarat Road – much to the satisfaction of the family. In turn they have sent us a copy of the Pryor family history. Mondays have been particularly busy with visitors coming to trace their forebears. Much interest has been generated form our website – http://home.vicnet.net.au/~newdhs/ and in recent weeks we have had callers from Queensland and NSW as well as closer to home. Unfortunately our reader-printer has been causing trouble and a backlog of articles still needs to be printed out from our microfilm copies of the local papers. To those of us at the courthouse on these occasions, the work put into indexing the local newspapers and the expense of buying the microfilms is worthwhile as we note the appreciation expressed by these visitors.
