NEWSLETTER MAY 2000

MAPS

On several occasions the need has arisen to have topographical maps of Newstead and the surrounding areas. At the May meeting, permission was granted to purchase and the Society now possesses copies of Newstead, Yandoit, Cairn Curran, Guildford, Castlemaine and Maldon.

CURRENT RESEARCH

Work is ongoing on the Heritage Walk for sites to be included in the brochure. It is on occasions like this that old beliefs are questioned. The origins of the name ”Newstead” are currently being investigated and this has involved Liz Coady going to the State Library to search old newspapers and Government Gazettes. Watch this space!

EXCURSION

As a result of a request for information, Barry and May Thornbury from Castlemaine are interested in the South Nuggetty Mine site which is close to the BIow Mine track. Also in this area are a few other mines mentioned by David Bannear in his book of mines in this area. Therefore it has been decided to go for an excursion on Monday afternoon next, 29th May meeting at the courthouse at 1.30 pm. Last year members seemed to enjoy these mini-excursions that we had to mines in the Welshmans Reef area, Thornhill Reef and Byron mines so it is to be hoped that this one proves just as popular.

SUBSCRIPTIONS

Thanks to the members who have paid their membership dues for the current year and a gentle reminder again for those who haven’t! Single membership $15 Family membership $25 plus $5 postage for those who have the newsletter posted.

MONDAY MORNINGS

It is very gratifying to see the number of people on a Monday morning at the Courthouse. On a typical morning, there is maintenance, research on the micro- film reader being undertaken, several people entering data from old newspapers onto computer as well as a person or two calling in to see what could be found in our archives on their fore-bears. Thank you to all our helpers!
