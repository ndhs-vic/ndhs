NOVEMBER 2008 NEWLETTER

 

MARKET REPORT

What a ripper! In the days leading up to the market, the organisers were despondent about our chances and there was even mutterings along the lines of ‘this might be our last market’. However the response to our distributions of raffle tickets for the raffle was wonderful, the weather turned out to be ideal and great publicity was arranged by Cherie Lawton. When the takings were tallied, it transpired that we made about $1,200 which is our best result ever. The ‘team’ seems to have their respective chores worked out well and thanks to their efforts and those of the broader community who provide the music, and who set up stalls or come, browse and patronise our sellers, the day went off well. The markets will continue for a while yet. Rotunda Park is such a wonderful setting and it is gratifying to see it being used by the community for the community.

The winner of the homemade fruitcake for the lucky stallholder was John the Glassman and winner of the Christmas Hamper was the Lawton family.

It is amazing how a sequence of separate isolated items can come together and create a picture of a place or people. So it has been with Isaac Smith and the Newstead Pound. An e-mail, a request for information in a newspaper, a visit from a couple from New South Wales searching for family and a chance meeting at the recent celebration for the 150th anniversary of the first mass at Sandon have come up with the following story.

 

From the Echo 3 .7.1898

A terrible sudden death occurred at Sandon on Monday. Mrs Alexandrina Smith, wife of Mr Isaac Smith was nearing her confinement and her husband left home to go to Newstead for a doctor. He had got halfway when his son overtook him with the news that Mrs Smith had expired. Dr. Eccles was immediately summoned, and at an enquiry subsequently held by Mr J Mackie J. P., gave it as his opinion that she had been progressing satisfactorily till her heart failed to beat, which together with syncope, caused death. The funeral took place on Wednesday, the remains being interred in Newstead cemetery. Deceased was 42 years of age and had borne twelve children, six of whom survive her.

 

From the Echo 27.3.1918

An old resident of Newstead, in the person of Mr Isaac Smith, passed away at his residence on Sunday morning last. Deceased, who would have reached 69 years today had resided in the Newstead district the greater part of his life and was an old and highly respected officer of the Shire Council, he having had charge of the pound. The funeral took place on Tuesday afternoon, the remains being interred in the Presbyterian portion of the Newstead Cemetery; the Rev. W. Macdermid being the officiating clergyman.

 

Smith/Millar: Seeking descendants of our great-grandparents, Isaac David Smith and Alexandrina (Millar) married in Fifeshire, Scotland, 1873. Migrated to Australia, had eight children: Lilias Mary, b 1874, Echuca, David, b 1976, Echuca, Elizabeth, b 1878 Casterton, Mary, b 1879, Loddon, Florence, b 1881, Chewton, Walter Alexander, b 1882 Chewton, Ann Rachel, b 1885 Chewton, Ethel May, b, 1888 Newstead.
