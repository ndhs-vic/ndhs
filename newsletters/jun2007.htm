JUNE 2007 NEWSLETTER

 

NEXT EXCURSION

The proposed excursion to follow up the theme of Water & Newstead and visit the old reservoir and the water races from the Jim Crowe, has been postponed due to difficulty of getting access to some of the sites due to the rain! How wonderful! Instead the proposal is to go into the Muckleford forest. The first stop will be the roasting kilns at Thornhill Reef. You may remember that our current president, Derek Reid, was responsible for getting a grant to have the stonework repaired at this important site. We make periodic visits to check on it and will take with us the relevant information so that we can reflect on its colourful past.

From there it will a chance to go further down Demo Track, into Blow Mine Track and then into Golf Links Track to finish up at the Red, White and Blue to have a cuppa. On the way we will pass the Blow Mine, Young Australia and the omega Reef workings so there will be plenty to see.

The arrangements are to leave the courthouse by car-pooling at 1.30 pm on Monday 2nd July weather permitting. If the weather looks dodgy you can ring 54762006 between 12.30 and 1.00 to check.

 

TOILET/STORAGE FACILITY

You will no doubt be less than surprised to know that work continues as usual – since the last newsletter we can report that there has been 2 days work done and the framework is now up.

The first run of 100 books has almost sold out and we are negotiating a second print run.

 

PHOTO BONANZA

The scanner and its operator have been working overtime this week, as we were fortunate to have Heather Barkla visit us with a collection of her father’s photos. As well as being a keen photographer himself, Harold Barkla had a wonderful collection of old family photos of the Barkla and related families. (We think the some of the old photos of World War 1 soldiers are of John Ritchie Wattie, the soldier whose grave was featured in the last newsletter). Already 150 have been scanned and documented and we think there are at least twice as many still to be done.

The range of subjects is incredible – family occasions such as weddings and visiting relations, district events that range from the 1927 Back To Newstead, the Sandon tornado etc to wonderful old studio photographs of the Barkla family of previous generations. Many family ones are taken on former properties such as Greenhill Farm so they will be of interest to many members and local residents.

It proves that – you never know who will come through the door of the courthouse on a Monday morning and what they will bring!

 

MORE RESEARCH

In answer to the request in last month’s Echo, we have another helper at the courthouse who is interested in indexing the local newspapers. So that means we now have 3 people working on the readers and doing the periods 1907, 1914 and 1920. Janet Trudgeon has been fascinated that in 1907, the American fleet visited Melbourne and caused considerable interest. There were details of how to see the fleet and a Castlemaine shop cashed in by advertising their sale of sheets and Manchester under the sign of “American Fleet Sale” decorated with the American flag! The 1914 editions have columns of world news with Mr Churchill, Minister for the Navy in Great Britain advocating that the navies of New Zealand and Australia be put under the command of the mother country, as there was to be a conflagration such as the world had never seen.
