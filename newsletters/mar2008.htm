MARCH NEWSLETTER 2008

 

NEWSTEAD MARKET

Thanks to all those who helped on Saturday 15th March - it was really a hard slog this time because of the hot conditions. We are pleased to report that the society’s funds have been increased by $600 and we all survived! The winner of the fruitcake for the lucky stallholder was Val Tangey and Dorothy Clark won the Easter raffle.

 

ANNUAL MEETING

April is annual meeting time and it has been traditional to have a guest speaker on this occasion. This year one of our members, Mellissa Conn from Guildford will talk about the history of the building that Mellissa and her husband own. Known as London House or more recently as the Goldfields Gallery, it has surrendered a lot of artefacts that Mellissa and Jason have on display in the shop front. The history of the building will be the subject of Mellissa’s talk and that will inevitably be the wider history of Guildford itself. It promises to be most interesting. Beforehand we will have a short meeting to elect new office bearers and afterwards there will be supper and a chance to socialise.

APRIL 21ST AT THE COURTHOUSE AT 8 PM.

 

ACQUISITIONS

The Society has 3 new books in our library. They are “Deeper Leads – New approaches in Victorian goldfields history” edited by Keir Reeves and David Nichols;

the recently published book on Findlay Campbell of Strathloddon by Marjorie Grant; “Writing Family History Made Easy “ by Noeline Kyle.

The Society has also been given a parish map of the Glengower settlement given to us by former resident Alan Nicholson. The map has the names of the settlers on their blocks. Alan has also given us a chart he has drawn up showing the families who lived on the blocks over the ensuing years and a document that shows how hard a struggle it was for the soldier settlers to pay off their farms.

 

COMPUTER

Another acquisition has been a superseded computer thanks to Mount Alexander Shire. This means that we can dispose of one of our old ‘dinosaurs’ and this one can be linked to our other two relatively modern ones. It will be able to have all our data on it and be available for researchers.

 

PRO AWARD

The archival material has been given to us that we won at the last Family History Expo at Ballarat. Public Records Office donates archival material as prizes for the best displays on the day and last year Newstead received an encouragement award of $100. We were able to choose the sort of material we wanted so our box of goodies included large plastic sleeves for maps, vinyl boxes for our microfilms and double-sided tape for our Mylar work. The material was presented at the last CHHA quarterly meeting and although car breakdown prevented our representative from attending, the material was delivered thanks to the kind offices of Ted and Beryl Maidment.

 

BROCHURES

Thanks to the efforts of Liz Coady, five brochures of walks around Newstead on themes of Floods, Law and Order, Sacred to Worship and Memory, CBD and Creating a Township were published several years ago.

The brochures have now been updated and are in the hands of Mount Alexander Shire who are putting together information on all the walks within the Shire. New copies for our district should be available soon.

 

DAVE CLARK

Our sympathy goes to the Clark family for the recent loss of Dave. Dave’s presence around the Society has been invaluable. Always cheerful, he was there to provide the music and announcements at the Markets and to accompany us on excursions particularly when we visited the RSL in Castlemaine. Dave has been our guest speaker and amazed us with memories of his wartime experiences. Luckily his story is captured in an oral history that is the process of being put into a printed format. He delighted to lend a hand when visiting children came and taught the intricacies of marble playing. We will miss him.

 

EXCURSIONS

Due to Market commitments and the heat, excursions have been in abeyance for the time being. However we have a couple of great ones planned – keep posted for details!

 

WOMEN’S SUFFRAGE PETITION

One of our members, Margaret Pekin, has offered to do some research on those women who signed the petition and were from the areas close to Newstead. Margaret said she did not live near enough to support us in a lot of our work but felt that this was one way of helping. We really appreciated the work that Margaret has done and our thanks go to her.

 

CALENDAR OF EVENTS

Annual meeting 21st April at the courthouse at 8 pm.

Research at the courthouse on Monday mornings – all welcome.
