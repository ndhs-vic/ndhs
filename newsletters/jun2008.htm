NEWSLETTER June 2008

 

EXCURSION

A wonderful treat was in store for those who ignored the cold winter weather for the June excursion. About nine cars took those who assembled at the courthouse on Monday June 9th, to Yapeen where Maurie Gervasoni was waiting for us. The first place to see was “Locarno” and as we drove past we were imagining the scene that Maurie had described to us, of the Sunday night card games in the early Yandoit days. We then travelled through Yandoit, seeing the Righetti house in the distance where there had been a creamery, up the hill past the site of the Yandoit racecourse to the Yandoit Creek area. Here a stop was made at the old Gervasoni house, “Bergamo”. Maurie showed us a photo of the house from Claude Culvenor’s book about the Gervasoni family. The photo was in stark contrast to the state of ruin the house is in today.  Despite its present day appearance, the house, outbuildings and cellar were still impressive and as Maurie described things such as the harvesting of the grapes and the process of winemaking in the fine old cellar, it was easy to imagine it as it was in its glory days – dance floor and all. Another stop was made at another of the stone houses, where yet another wine cellar was inspected with its many nails in the ceiling for hanging bullboar sausages. Here a massive beam in the kitchen was pointed out – part of the timber that had been hand cut, much of which was brought down from the bush on makeshift wire “flying fox” type arrangements.

A stop was made for a cup of tea and a vote of thanks made to Maurie before we traversed the Old coach Road and somehow wound our way through the bush and found ourselves back in Yandoit again.

The day reminded us again of the debt we owe the pioneers of the district – this time the Swiss-Italians of Yandoit.

 

WORKING BEE

Monday 1st June was the date for the working bee to plant a screen of shrubs and trees along our southern boundary fence-line and around the toilet/storage building. Thanks are expressed to Frances Cincotta of Newstead Natives who kindly donated the plants and to the volunteers who provided the man-power. We now plan to obtain some mulch to finish the project.

 

RESEARCH

We have been quite snowed under with requests for information that have come from Canada, Queensland, NSW and places closer to home. Much of this involves finding and printing articles from our collection of local newspapers on microfilm. The parlous state of our second-hand reader-printer is of concern and there is a need for a better machine. Much time and effort is now going into checking out the various machines available before investigating our chances of getting a grant to help finance the purchase.

Meantime we work on – with requests from the families of Pryor, Clarey, Smith, McDonald, Matheson and Jenkin. In nearly every case, we are delighted to receive in return, the family histories and stories from the people we have been able to help.
