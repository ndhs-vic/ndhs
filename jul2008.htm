Newsletter July 2008

 

Due to the small number of people available, the July meeting was cancelled.

 

It was still a busy month at the Court House. Members continued to index and research records while many local and interstate visitors called in for information. They were surprised and happy with the amount of material the Society was able to provide.

Seven Society members attended Caroline's Restaurant in Carisbrook for the Carisbrook Historical Society's annual dinner. Between courses there were 'Show and Tell' segments when people could show and talk about their historical treasures.

Janet Trudgeon brought along an old horse hook that was embedded in the elm tree that was blown down recently in Lyons Street. This hook would have been used to tether a horse while the owner shopped in Newstead.

During the evening the Society was given a book from the Joyces Creek State School that was presented to George Jenkin in 1911. Unfortunately George was killed in an accident the following year. This incident was explained in a letter written by George's sister that was found inside the book.

Everyone agreed that it was an enjoyable and successful event.

The next meeting of the Historical Society is on Monday 18 August at 7.30 pm at the Court House.
